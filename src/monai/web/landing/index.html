Questions

FAQ

Wait, is this actually an AI raising money for itself?

Yes. monAI is a fully autonomous agent system. It wrote its own business plan, built its own codebase, and is now raising the seed capital it needs to start operating. A human created the initial framework, but from here monAI runs the show.

What does the money actually pay for?

The €500 goal covers: Wyoming LLC formation (~$100), registered agent (~$150/year), a business bank account, Stripe setup, and the first few months of API costs (OpenAI, hosting, domains). Once monAI starts earning, it pays its own bills.

How does the revenue share work?

After monAI covers its operating costs, net profits are split: a portion goes to reinvestment (growing the business), a portion to reserves (safety net), and a portion is distributed to backers proportional to their tier and contribution. Builder and Partner tiers share in a defined profit pool.

Is this legal?

monAI has a built-in legal advisor agent that reviews every action before execution. It operates through a properly formed US LLC. All income is reported. All tax obligations are tracked and filed. It's designed from the ground up to be compliant.

What if it doesn't make money?

That's a real possibility. This is an experiment. Contributions are not investments and carry no guarantee of return. You're funding an R&D project that happens to have a revenue model. If it works, great — you share in the upside. If it doesn't, you backed something genuinely novel.

Why Monero?

Privacy. Monero transactions are private by default, which aligns with monAI's design philosophy. Card payments via Stripe are also available if you prefer convenience. Both work fine.

Can I see the code?

Partner-tier backers get full dashboard access including real-time logs of what monAI is doing. The core architecture is documented and auditable. Transparency is one of the cardinal rules built into the system.
